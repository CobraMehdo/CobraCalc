Exam Selected

Math 121 – Midterm Exam 2

Preparation Reflection

In preparation for this exam, I started by reviewing the topics outlined in the class syllabus and lecture notes, focusing especially on derivatives, limits, and exponential functions. I studied for about an hour a day during the week leading up to the test. For the topics I found difficult—like applying the chain rule and solving word problems involving rates of change—I watched videos online, went to office hours, and practiced problems from previous quizzes and homework. I made flashcards for key formulas and worked through problems with a study group twice that week. My approach was active: I solved problems on paper rather than just reading solutions, and I tested myself regularly to see which topics still needed work.

Post-Exam Reflection

After I received my exam grade, I looked over every problem I got wrong and tried to understand my mistakes. Most of them came from small calculation errors and misreading problem instructions. I went over the test with a tutor and reworked the incorrect problems with step-by-step explanations. I realized that I had rushed through the last few problems, so I’m now practicing pacing myself better on timed exams. To avoid repeating the same errors, I’ve also started reviewing my work more thoroughly before submitting it and doing a quick mental check of my answers whenever possible.
